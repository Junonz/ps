We’ve made a pack of changes

We’re making some changes to the way we price our power to make it simpler and easier to understand. The fixed costs that were previously included within unit prices are being replaced by fixed daily charges.

We’ve also made some changes to how we display Powerpacks and your usage in our mobile app. It’s all about making it easier to see the value of using Powershop. Watch the video to get a run-down of the changes.

Balance Screen

Dollars or days ahead

Inside the circle on the Balance screen we now tell you about how many dollars ahead or behind you are with the power you have bought, compared to the power you have used.

Tap the circle to see about how many days ahead or behind you are.

The Shop

The bottom line

Beside each Powerpack below the circle we now tell you:

  • The cost of the pack (what you pay for it), and how much it’s worth (based on your Standard Power price). This makes it easier to see what you’re saving. Extra-special special packs (like your monthly Simple Saver) will also show you a percentage discount off Standard Power. Making the savings simpler!

  • The cost of the pack is also shown in dollars per day, based on your current usage. Dollars per day is the new way to compare the value of different packs at the time you buy them. It also helps you budget.

BACK TO TOP

Powerpack details

Tap each pack to go to the Powerpack details screen, where we’ll tell you how many days of power the pack should give you based on your current usage (which can change from day to day). You can also see this in the circle by adding the pack to your cart.

Keeping it fresh

We’ve freshened up our old Powerpacks and created some new ones, in different sizes to give you more choice.

Powerpacks no longer have expiry dates.
Fresher for longer!

Future Packs now come with a little discount – so you can save as well as spread your power costs. The earlier you buy a Future Pack, the more you will save.

Insights

Knowledge is power

Below your Daily usage graphs we now tell you the dollar cost per day, and the kWh (kilowatt hours) of energy used per day. We don’t talk about units or cents per unit anymore.

The bars in the Insights graph also let you see at a glance the monthly dollar cost of your power.
